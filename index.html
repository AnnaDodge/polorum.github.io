What We Do

Last summer, while the Ebola outbreak wrought havoc in West Africa, another disease outbreak occurred in a rural community in East Africa. Typhoid was prevelant, but few locals new about it - knew that their water might be unsafe. In the first three months of the outbreak awareness was at only 15% in the local community. Between July and November 2014 there were 600 typhoid infections and 46 deaths recorded by the single hospital serving 12,000 people. The devastation caused by the disease was worsened by the fact that the hospital had no method of communicating news of the outbreak to those it serves. ZvakanakaSMS have combined technical expertise with knowledge of rural Africa to build a cost-effective solution to this problem by capitalising on the high regional mobile phone ownership. We are aware that not every day is an emergency, or disease outbreak, and our system can be productively used for valuable - but much more mundane - messages. Such as indicating the time and location of a community meeting, or selling advertising messages for sole traders based in the community. Our system can be run by locals, providing individuals with employment, and ensuring that the information will be trusted if and when disaster strikes.


The system

Our system is extremely cheap. It can be run without internet access, and without mains electricity. It fits into pre-existing infrastructure. Our software can hold over 40,000 phone numbers and complies with data protection regulations. We can also provide the technical expertise required to set-up a system in a community thereby allowing information to be transmitted in hours rather than months. There is a need for what we are doing; a simple solution that can be instantly implemented and accepted by the communities running the system.
