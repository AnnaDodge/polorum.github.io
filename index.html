What We Do

Last summer, while the Ebola outbreak wreaked havoc in West Africa, another disease outbreak occurred in a rural community in East Africa. The disease was typhoid and in the first three months of the outbreak awareness was at only 15% in the local community. Between July and November 2014 there were 600 typhoid infections and 46 deaths recorded by the single hospital serving 12,000 people. The devastation wreaked by the disease was augmented by the fact that the hospital had no method of communicating news of the outbreak to its benefactors. Coupling our technical expertise with knowledge of rural Africa ZvakanakaSMS have built a cost- effective solution to the problem of disseminating potentially life-saving information to communities by capitalising on the high regional mobile phone ownership.We are aware that not every day is an emergency, or disease outbreak; however, there is no barrier to sending other types of information such as indicating the time and location of a community meeting, or selling advertising messages for sole traders based in the community. Our system can be run by locals, providing individuals with employment, and ensuring that the information will be trusted if and when disaster strikes.

About us

By selling directly to partner organizations, who lack the technical expertise we can provide, ZvakanakaSMS can provide support.
